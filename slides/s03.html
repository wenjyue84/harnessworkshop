The Engine vs. The Car

💡 Feynman Explanation

A raw V8 engine produces enormous power — but alone on the ground, it just spins and explodes. The car is the system that makes that power useful and directed.

Raw Power ⚙

The V8 Engine

= The LLM

Massive horsepower, rapid probabilistic generation. Without constraint, it hallucinates, contradicts, and crashes your codebase.

  • Infinite creativity (good)
  • No memory or identity
  • No built-in discipline
  • Defaults to "average" output
VS
Control System 🚗

The Car

= The Harness

The chassis, steering wheel, and brakes. claude.md, CLIs, hooks, and precise context boundaries safely direct that raw power.

  • claude.md → persistent identity
  • CLIs → typed action contracts
  • Hooks → guardrail gates
  • Memory → long-term state
🏭 The Paradigm Shift

Stop trying to build a better engine.
Start building a better car to handle the engine you already have.
